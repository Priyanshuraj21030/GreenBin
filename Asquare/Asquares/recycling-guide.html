Recycling Materials Guide

Plastics

Understanding plastic recycling numbers (1-7):

Do Recycle:
  • PET bottles (Type 1)
  • HDPE containers (Type 2)
  • Clean plastic containers
Don't Recycle:
  • Plastic bags
  • Styrofoam
  • Contaminated containers

Paper & Cardboard

Do Recycle:
  • Newspapers & magazines
  • Cardboard boxes
  • Office paper
Don't Recycle:
  • Greasy pizza boxes
  • Wax-coated paper
  • Used paper towels

The Recycling Process

Collection

Materials are collected from homes and businesses

Sorting

Items are separated by material type

Processing

Materials are cleaned and prepared for recycling

Manufacturing

Recycled materials become new products

Detailed Process Overview

Collection Phase:
  • Curbside pickup from households
  • Drop-off at recycling centers
  • Business and commercial collection
Sorting Phase:
  • Manual sorting of large items
  • Mechanical sorting using machines
  • Quality control checks

Common Recycling Mistakes

Contaminated Materials
  • Not rinsing containers before recycling
  • Recycling food-soiled items
  • Including greasy pizza boxes
Tip: Always clean containers before recycling
Wrong Items
  • Including plastic bags in recycling bin
  • Mixing different types of materials
  • Adding non-recyclable plastics
Tip: Check local recycling guidelines
Incorrect Sorting
  • Not separating different materials
  • Mixing paper with containers
  • Including non-recyclable items
Tip: Sort materials properly before recycling
Size Issues
  • Recycling items that are too small
  • Not breaking down large boxes
  • Shredding paper too finely
Tip: Follow size guidelines for recyclables
